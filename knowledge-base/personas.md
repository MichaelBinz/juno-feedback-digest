# User Personas — Juno Feedback Digest

Three primary personas have been identified for the Juno Feedback Digest. All tasks involving user stories, PRD content, or UX considerations should reference these personas.

---

## Persona 1: Platform Lead

**Name:** Petra  
**Role:** Platform Engineering Lead  
**Team:** Internal Developer Platform, Dynatrace R&D  

### Background
Petra leads a team of 6 platform engineers responsible for the internal developer toolchain at Dynatrace. She owns the developer experience roadmap and is accountable for platform adoption metrics. She attends quarterly planning sessions where she needs to justify priorities with data.

### Goals
- Understand which platform pain points are most frequently raised by developers
- Prioritise engineering investments based on real developer signal, not loudest voices
- Demonstrate to leadership that the platform team is responsive to developer needs
- Reduce the time her team spends manually triaging feedback in Slack

### Frustrations
- Feedback arrives in 12+ Slack channels — impossible to monitor all of them
- Most impactful feedback is buried after a few hours of Slack scroll
- Hard to distinguish between one-off complaints and systemic issues
- No structured way to track whether issues have been resolved

### How She Uses the Digest
- Reads the Monday digest over her morning coffee
- Uses the top-3 themes section to prepare for weekly team planning
- Shares the digest with engineering leads to align on priorities
- Flags high-priority items for immediate follow-up

### Quote
> "I don't need every piece of feedback — I need the *right* pieces, at the right time, in a format I can act on."

## Persona 2: Developer Advocate

**Name:** Daniel  
**Role:** Developer Advocate  
**Team:** Juno DevRel  

### Background
Daniel is part of the Juno DevRel team. He creates documentation, runs workshops, produces video content, and is active in Slack communities. He represents the "voice of the developer" inside Dynatrace and uses community feedback to decide what to create next.

### Goals
- Stay on top of what developers are struggling with, asking about, and celebrating
- Identify themes for blog posts, documentation updates, and workshop content
- Surface positive feedback to celebrate wins and share with the broader team
- Catch confusion and frustration early before it becomes disengagement

### Frustrations
- He can't read every Slack message — he relies on colleagues tagging him, which is inconsistent
- Survey data arrives in batches (post-release) and is often stale by the time he acts on it
- Hard to connect qualitative feedback to quantitative NPS trends
- No single view of sentiment across different developer communities

### How He Uses the Digest
- Uses the theme section to plan his content calendar for the week
- Pulls quotes for use in blog posts and presentations (with permission)
- Shares sentiment trends with the broader DevRel team in their weekly sync
- Flags documentation gaps identified in feedback for the docs team

### Quote
> "Developers tell us exactly what they need — we just need to be listening in all the right places at the same time."

## Persona 3: Product Manager

**Name:** Maria  
**Role:** Senior Product Manager  
**Team:** Juno Product, Dynatrace R&D  

### Background
Maria owns the product roadmap for Juno. She works closely with engineering, DevRel, and senior stakeholders to decide what gets built, when, and why. She is data-driven and holds herself accountable to measurable outcomes. She runs quarterly roadmap reviews and needs to defend prioritisation decisions with evidence.

### Goals
- Track how developer sentiment changes across product releases
- Identify feature requests that appear repeatedly and deserve roadmap consideration
- Measure the impact of product changes on developer satisfaction over time
- Spend less time manually gathering feedback for quarterly reviews

### Frustrations
- NPS surveys are infrequent and don't capture in-the-moment reactions
- Qualitative feedback is hard to aggregate and present to leadership
- Difficult to distinguish product feedback from support issues and general noise
- No historical trend data — each feedback review starts from scratch

### How She Uses the Digest
- Reviews sentiment trends week-over-week to track the impact of releases
- Extracts feature request patterns to feed into quarterly planning
- Exports digest data for inclusion in roadmap review decks
- Uses the flagged items section to identify urgent issues requiring product response

### Quote
> "I need a system that turns the river of developer feedback into a signal I can put in front of a VP and say: this is what we need to fix."
